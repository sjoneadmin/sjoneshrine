Who We Serve

Sjòne Shrine is a specialized after-school program designed for adolescent women ages 18–25 (with situational exceptions) who are entering adulthood without guidance or support.

Whether you're the daughter of immigrants, recently aged out of support systems, or simply finding your way without guidance — we're here to meet you at the start line.

Our Approach

We offer a structured, dignified path forward in clinical, cyber, and self-empowerment disciplines — combining care, technology, and identity work — all under one roof.

Our mission is simple:

To prevent homelessness, exploitation, isolation, and systemic exclusion by establishing community with a focus on real-world survival and growth.

Our Program

Because our program is intentionally small, highly personalized, and ceremonial, we accept four applicants through a thoughtful, once-every-four-years review process as of right now.

You're Still Welcome

Broader Community Access

Applicants not chosen for the top four are still invited to enter the broader Shrine community. We offer support services on a rolling basis — including workshops, remote guidance, and check-ins — and you may be next in line as future opportunities open up.

Ongoing Resources

We also provide ongoing resources including:

  • •
    Safety reporting channels to keep you protected
  • •
    Down payment assistance for stable housing
  • •
    College tuition fund support for remaining balances

Disclaimer

Access to these broader services is dependent on available funding. While we aim to serve as many as we can, priority is given to those most at risk, and offerings may vary based on current resources.
